Creating Sourced Users

Creating sourced users was fairly straightforward. Clicking the "Add Person" button within directory > Users and filling out the form just like any other administration application.

Solution:

I would provide our clients with phone numbers for their individual marketing campaigns.

  • Using five9's IVA (inteligent voice automation), I developed over 100 custom IVA routes for over 30 different clients.
  • Each route would define a lead source variable for every call dialed to that route.
  • The lead source variable would attach to the inbound connector as a parameter for our Sales Rep's scripting form.
  • The scripting form would dynamically fill the lead source question completely based on which phone number the customer called.

So what?

The following were the results of the project.

  • Sales Reps were happier that they had one less section to go through in the call flow.
  • Clients were delighted to see minutes shaved from every call thanks to this automation.
  • Customersnever had to spend time remembering how they heard about the client.

Chrome Browser Cloud Management

No AD? No MDM? No Group Policy? No Problem!

Enforced policies on the browser that doesn't require users to be logged into chrome.

Problem:

Previous system was a shared google account that wasn't in it's own OU in Google Workspace. We could set security policies for the shared account which would apply to 700+ employees sharing the account.

  • Chrome would have consistent issues with syncing. Problem occurs when 700+ employees on the shared account are logging in and out simultaneously.
  • Security was a critical risk factor since a massive amount of employees shared one google account.
  • Logistical and budget limitations prevented non-support staff from obtaining their own work email through google workspace hence the singe shared account.

Solution:

I was the principle architect for structured OUs in Google Workspace. Utilized google's Managed Browser feature to eliminate the need for end users to log into a shared account to receive security policies. This implimentation also allowed us to stay within the logistical and budget limitations imposed on this particular problem.

  • Set security polices in the OU instead of the shared account.
  • Created an enrolment token which was placed in our cloning software's golden image.
  • Our cloning software, Clonezilla, would automatically enroll the device in the Managed Browser since the enrollment token was applied in the golden image.

Users could sync chrome with their own personal google accounts, but our security policies would apply regardless. It's much like having our own company web browser that's chrome based. Except we decide what the browser can and can't do. For companies with a BYOD environment and all web-based applications, this solution is a perfect fit!
